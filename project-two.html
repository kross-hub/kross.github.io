The Challenge
‍

The task at hand was to design a cloud storage platform that catered to an untapped market. There is an already saturated cloud storage market , the only way to find a unique approach would be to ask the users who benefit from such a resource. In addition to finding a new approach to making a name for ourselves in the cloud storage universe our client was very concerned with the lack of logical organization that appeared in current cloud storage options. The viable solution resulted in our creation of ShelfSpace, a one stop shop for all the users cloud storage needs, including viable organization options and enhanced social collaboration.

The Solution
‍

We present ShelfSpace, a website which seamlessly allows users to combine all aspects of cloud storage with the benefits that you receive on a social platform. Shelfspace allows storage for all types of files (docs, photos, links, events and creative ideas) with an advanced social platform. This allows users to share and collaborate on a public or private forum with friends, family, and like minded people. Lastly, the platform supports optimal organization allowing files to be pre-organized in either a public or private space depending on the users preferences.

Research

ShelfSpace was a project I completed during my time at bloc. I led from start to finish with guidance from a mentor. After the initial idea was concepted, I began with a competitive analysis and user survey. This research showed an opportunity for a cloud service site on the market, so I moved forward developinguser personas, and the the foundation of the websites design and features.

Users had a variety of goals they wanted to achieve: being able to organize all files easily, being able to use only one site, and combining their cloud service with the social sphere. When creating user stories, each goal was given attention and incorporated into the design. User flows also reflected these goals, and outlined a structure for users to easily navigate the app.

SITEMAP

Based on the insights gained from the initial content audit and competitor analysis with potential users, I defined the sitemap for Shelfspace and then evaluated it via tree tests with potential users.

WIREFRAMES

With low-fidelity Wireframes, the general structure of the website could easily be tested in usability tests. Without much effort, adjustments could be made before going into the much more time consuming digital implementation. It was found that adjustments to the navigation bar needed to be altered to create an intuitive navigation system. Furthermore, alterations to the “how to use the site” were found to be necessary.

BRAND AND STYLE GUIDE

As I was beginning on wireframes for ShelfSpace, I also developed a brand and style guide. The logo represents the combination of a social space and cloud storage service users were looking for. For typography, I used for PT-Serrif its geometric design and Open Sans for clear presentation. I wanted the color pallette to be warm and outdoorsy and was drawn to colors that reminded me of the pleasure of being outdoors.

TESTING AND ITERATIONS

Usability tests and metrics revealed that some of the process and design decisions I made were not as intuitive as I thought. Referring back to the personas, I knew that users heavily value simple organization and for their needs to be met within one site.As a result, I needed to iterate, test, and achieve measurable outcomes to overcome these product shortcomings.

Challenge One

Feedback

  • Confusion on how to navigate the "how to use Shelfspace"
  • "How do you know if steps on how to use the site have been completed?"

Initial Design

Solution

  • Clarified which steps needed to be completed during the introduction to the site.
  • Added easily identified symbols to represent each step.

Final Design

Challenge Two

Feedback

  • Confusion on where the completed sites came from.
  • "How do you know they actually did a trip to this site and didn't just leave a review.

Initial Design

Solution

  • New wording to inform purpose
  • Easily identified tab bar

Final Design

Challenge Three

Feedback

  • Confusion on where did this photo come from.
  • "How do I add another photo to this shelf?"

Initial Design

Solution

  • Added a empty state when entering a shelf.
  • Easy to follow directions on how to add a new item to a shelf.

Final Design

What did i learn?

It was a rewarding experience to design a product from scratch. Though the direction wasn’t clear in the beginning, each step added clarity and helped me to get closer to the final prototype. While there are still a lot of possibilities to explore to combine the social sphere with a functioning cloud service, I believe this is a good start. A few insights during the usability tests were particularly surprising to me. Some of the elements which I thought were intuitive were found to be pain points. However, eventually, the testing and feedback allowed me to design an even better solution catered to the user’s pain points. I learned that research and testing are vital to completing any project. If I had more time, I would have considered doing more research at different steps in the process.
